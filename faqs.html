Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)



What can electrolysis be used to treat?

Originally when electrolysis was invented, its purpose was to remove those irritating, in-grown eyelash hairs. After over 100 years, electrolysis has been expanded to treat more areas of the body, such as the face, thighs, abdomen, breasts, legs, and more!


Is electrolysis safe?

Absolutely! Electrolysis is a very safe method of hair removal, unlike depilatories or bleaches, which carry harmful chemicals.


Are there any permanent side effects?

Nope! Sometimes, a slight reddening of the skin occurs during or immediately after treatment, but this only lasts for a short time.


Is electrolysis painful?

The truth is, electrolysis usually does not cause much discomfort. Modern electrolysis methods have reduced the discomfort to a mere tingling. A topical anesthetic may be used in some cases.


Aren't the DIY electric tweezers just as effective for permanent hair removal?

The FDA (Food and Drug Administration) and the AMA (American Medical Association) recognize only electrolysis as a permanent method of removing hair. In fact, some states even prohibit electric tweezers from being advertised as a method of providing permanent hair removal.
